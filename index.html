Previous Education, Career
and how I became a SWE

My education starts when I graduated from the University of Arizona with a Bachelor’s degree in engineering management with a focus in civil engineering and mathematics minor. After completing my undergraduate degree, I started working for a major oil and gas company as a wellsite field engineer. As a field engineer, I managed wellsite operations for logging gamma radiation wellbore data in real time, I was the onsite consultant for technical troubleshooting and I served as a liaison between the client representative on site and my supervisors in the office.

After a couple of years, I decided to pursue a Master’s degree in Geophysics where I was re-introduced to programming via MatLab, which I used to analyze seismic data for my thesis. After graduating with my Master’s, I started working again for another oil and gas company, and while this job as a well fracturing field engineer was less physically demanding, the work/life balance and work culture was still not what I wanted for myself in the long run. So, I decided to pursue a career in what I initially studied for by taking the Fundamentals of Engineering Exam. After studying for this 6 hour exam in a few short months, I passed the test and became a licensed Engineer in Training with the state of Texas. While I was finishing a graduate certificate program at Texas Tech University, the Covid pandemic started and essential shut the construction industry down. While I was considering whether I should wait for the market to turn around, I kept myself busy with productive hobbies, mainly programming.

During the shut down I decided to teach myself Python and I started to research the job market for software development. After some research, I discovered that there is a severe workforce shortage of software developers in the US and I started to do further research on how to get into the tech industry. After some time, I came across App Academy and their impressive reviews. Once I was accepted into their program, I completed an immersive 6 month bootcamp where I learned how to create a fullstack web application using Javascript, Python, React, Redux, Flask, SQLite and SQLAlchemy, HTML, CSS, Dockerfile and Heroku as a website hosting platform. I will be a great candidate for your company because my journey to becoming a software engineer has shown my dedication to learning and seeking a career that I can look back on and be proud of. I also know how to interact with people from various educational backgrounds, so I’m used to explaining technical jargon to a non-technical audience. My initial reason for considering software development was because I did not feel like I was fully utilizing the knowledge I’ve acquired through years of formal education. While my previous position required critical thinking and problem solving skills, the job overall never changed and there was not a lot of room for innovation. As a software engineer for your company, I am ready to dive into this industry with an open mind for innovation and improving the lives of everyday people one app at a time.
